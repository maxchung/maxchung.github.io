Using text messaging in an emergency.

Man and woman walking on a trail.
You see your friend slip and fall on a trail.
Your friend on the ground is not moving.
You bend down to check on her.
She is bleeding from a cut on her head.
You shake her, but she does not respond.

Cell phone appears.
You open the phone app.
Phone app opens. There is a phone keypad.
You press 911. Then you tap the call button.
(keypad sounds)

The call is being made.
(dial tone)
An operator sees details about you.
Your mom added your phone number and personal information on a website.
The operator now knows you want to talk with simple words and clear steps.

An operator answers the call.

911. Do you need (static) help?
Yes (static).
Your call has ended.

The place has low signal strength.
The operator tried to call back, but failed.
You get a text message from the operator.
(alert sounds)
Text message alert appears.
You tap the alert to open it.

Text message app opens. There is one message.
The operator asks: "911. Do you need help?"
You tap the mic button on the keyboard. Then you say your reply.
You reply: "Yes."
You tap the send button. You do the same thing for each question.
The operator asks: "Do you need police, fire, or medical?"
You reply: "Medical."
The operator asks: "Where are you?"
You reply: "I am on the C-A-M-O-S-U-N Bog Trail. Period."
The operator asks: "Is that in Vancouver?"
You reply: "Yes."

An operator sees your location on a map.
From the location you said, the operator now knows where you are.

Screen shows text chat again.
The operator asks: "Is your phone number 604-473-9363?"
You reply: "Yes."
The operator asks: "Are you Nathan Frost?"
You reply: "Yes."

Your chat is handed off to someone else.
The operator asks: "Vancouver medical. What happened?"
You reply: "My friend fell and hit her head. Period."
The operator asks: "Is your friend awake and breathing?"
You do not know some words. You select the text and tap speak.
Your phone says: "Is your friend awake and breathing?"
You reply: "She is breathing but not awake. Period. There is blood on her
head. Period."
The operator asks: "Is there a lot of blood?"
You reply: "No."
The operator asks: "How old is your friend?"
You reply: "She is 25. Period."
The operator says: "Okay, help is coming."
The operator says: "Can you add a video of her head? Tap the camera button
by the text box."
You tap the camera button by the text box.

The camera app opens. You see the floor from the back camera.
You switch to the video mode.
You tap the red button to take a video of your friend's head.
(keypad sound)
(breathing sounds)
You tap again to finish.
(keypad sound)
You tap the use video button.

Screen shows text chat again.
You see the video you took.
You tap the send button.
Phone screen shows the file is being sent.
The operator looks at the video.
The operator says: "I see the video. Press on the wound with a cloth."
Some words are hard. You select the text and tap speak.
Your phone says: "I see the video. Press on the wound with a cloth."
You reply: "OK."
The operator says: "Help is coming. I will send you a message if I
have questions."

Image of flashing lights.
(siren sounds)
