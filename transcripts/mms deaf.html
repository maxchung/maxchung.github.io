Using text messaging in an emergency.

Man and woman walking on a trail.
Imagine you are walking down a trail with your friend when she slips and falls.
Friend on ground is unconscious.
You bend down to check on her.
She is bleeding from a cut on her head.
You shake her, but she does not respond.

Smartphone appears.
You open the phone dialer app.
Phone dialer app opens. There is a phone keypad.
You dial 911 and press the call button.
(keypad tones)

The call is being made.
(calling sound)
An emergency operator sees your medical ID profile.
You added your phone number and medical information in an emergency
caller database.
The emergency operator now knows you are deaf and prefer texting.

Screen shows active call again.
The emergency operator sends you a text message.
(phone vibrates)
Text message notification appears.
You open the notification to see the message.

Text message app opens. There is one message.
The operator asks: "911, do you need police, fire or medical?"
You reply with the onscreen keyboard.
You reply, "Medical."
You tap send to submit your reply. The process repeats for each question.
The operator asks: "Where is your emergency?"
You reply: "I am 10 minutes in on the Camosun Bog Trail starting on
19th Street and Camosun."
The operator asks: "Is that in Vancouver?"
You reply: "Yes."

An emergency operator sees your location on a map.
From the location you said, the operator now knows where you are.

Screen shows text chat again.
The operator asks: "Is your phone number (604) 473-9363?"
You reply: "Yes."
The operator asks: "Are you Nathan Frost?"
You reply: "Yes."

Your call is transferred to a medical call dispatcher.
The operator asks: "Vancouver Medical. What is the nature of your emergency?"
You reply: "My friend fell and hit her head."
The operator asks: "Is your friend awake and breathing?"
You reply: "She is breathing but unconscious. She is bleeding from a cut
on her head."
The operator asks: "Is she bleeding a lot?"
You reply: "The bleeding is slow."
The operator asks: "How old is your friend?"
You reply: "She is 25."
The operator says: "Okay I have dispatched an ambulance and they will
be there soon. Meanwhile, can you attach a video showing the wound?"

You tap the camera button by the message input box.
Camera app opens. View of back camera is visible.
You switch from photo to video mode.
You tap the record button to take a video of your friend lying on the walkway.
(key tone)
(heavy breaths)
You tap the record button again to stop recording.
(key tone)
To attach the video you tap the use video button.

Screen shows, text chat again. One video is attached.
You tap the send button.
Screen shows file upload progress.
Attached video appears in chat thread.
The emergency operator looks at the video.
The operator says: "I see the video. Apply pressure to the wound with
a handkerchief."
You reply: "Will do."
The operator says: "Help us on the way. If we need more information,
I will send you another message."

Image of flashing emergency lights.
Two minutes later, help comes.
