Using Speech-to-Speech Relay in an emergency.

Mom and son talking in a living room. Son is in a wheelchair.
Imagine you are in an electric wheelchair and you cannot move any of
your limbs.
You were talking to your mother when she suddenly collapsed.
Mother fallen over on sofa with hand on chest. She is in pain.
You call out to her, but she does not respond.

Back of man's head with headrest buttons and wheelchair-mounted smartphone.
Mouth joystick is hidden.
Smartphone appears.
On the home screen, you select the speed dial shortcut to call the
speech-to-speech relay.
The call is being made.
(dial tone beeping)

A relay answers the call.
The relay asks: "BC Speech-to-Speech. CA 1234. Area code and number to
dial please."
You say: "911."
The relay says: "Please hold while I dial 911."
An emergency operator answers the call.
The operator asks: "911. Do you need police, fire or medical?"
The relay says: "Speech-to-Speech Relay CA 1234. The caller will speak, and
I will repeat their words. Caller, you may begin."
You say: "Medical."
The relay re-voices your responses.
The relay says: "Medical."
The operator asks: "Where is your emergency?"
You say: "I am at 3999 Henning Drive, Burnaby."
The relay says: "3999 Heather Drive?"
You say: "No, Henning Drive."
The relay says: "Can you spell that for me please?"
You say, "H-E-N-N-I-N-G."
The relay says: "I am at 3999. Henning Drive, Burnaby."
The operator asks: "Which floor are you on?"
You say: "Ground floor."
The relay says: "Ground floor."

An emergency operator sees your location on a map. From the location data
sent by your smartphone, the emergency operator confirms your location.

Screen shows active call again.
The operator asks: "Relay, is your call back number, 877-673-4636?"
The relay says, "Yes."
The operator asks: "What is your first and last name?"
You say: "Nathan Frost."
The relay says: "Nathan Frost."

Your call is transferred to a medical call dispatcher.
The operator asks: "Burnaby Medical. What is the nature of your emergency?"
You say: "I was speaking with my mother when she suddenly fell down and
stopped moving."
The relay says: "I was speaking with my mother when... Can you repeat after
that please?"
You say: "She suddenly fell down and stopped moving."
The relay says: "I was speaking with my mother when she suddenly fell down
and stopped moving."
The operator asks: "Is your mother breathing?"
You say: "No."
The relay says: "No."
The operator asks: "How old is your mother?"
You say: "She is 62."
The relay says: "She is 62."
The operator says: "Okay, I have dispatched an ambulance and they will
be there soon. Meanwhile, I will stay on the line in case there are any
new developments."

Image of flashing emergency lights.
Two minutes later, help comes.
