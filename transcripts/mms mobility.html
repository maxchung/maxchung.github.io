Using text messaging in an emergency.

Man in electric wheelchair and woman walking on a trail.
Imagine you are in an electric wheelchair and you cannot move any of your limbs.
You see your friend slip and fall on a trail.
Friend on ground is unconscious.
You call out to her, but she does not respond.
You see her head is bleeding.

Back of man's head with headrest buttons and wheelchair-mounted smartphone.
Mouth joystick is hidden.
Smartphone appears.
You unlock your smartphone and open the phone dialer app with your
mouth joystick.
Phone dialer app opens. There is a phone keypad.
You dial 9-1-1 and press the call button.
(keypad tones)

The call is being made.
(ringing sound)
An emergency operator answers the call.
911. Do you need police (static), fire, or medical? (static)
(alert sound)
Your call has ended.

The area has bad reception.
The emergency operator tried to call back, but failed.
The emergency operator sends you a text message.
(alert tone)
Text message notification appears.
You open the notification to see the message.

Text message app opens. There is one message.
The operator asks: "911. Do you need police, fire or medical?"
You tap the microphone button on the keyboard to switch to voice dictation.
Then you speak your response.
You reply: "Medical."
You tap send to submit your reply. The process repeats for each question.
The operator asks: "Where is your emergency?"
You reply: "I am 10 minutes in on the C-A-M-O-S-U-N Bog Trail starting on
19th Street and C-A-M-O-S-U-N period."
The operator asks: "Is that in Vancouver?"
You reply: "Yes."

An emergency operator sees your location on a map.
From the location you said, the operator now knows where you are.

Screen shows text chat again.
The operator asks: "Is your phone number 604-473-9363?"
You reply: "Yes."
The operator asks: "What is your first and last name?"
You reply: "Nathan Frost."

Your call is transferred to a medical call dispatcher.
The operator asks: "Vancouver medical. What is the nature of your emergency?"
You reply: "My friend fell and hit her head period."
The operator asks: "Is your friend awake and breathing?"
You reply: "She is unconscious and there is blood on her head period.
I cannot check if she is breathing as I am in a wheelchair period."
The operator asks: "How old is your friend?"
You reply: "She is 25 period."
The operator says: "Okay, I have dispatched an ambulance and they will
be there soon. Meanwhile, can you attach a video showing the wound?"
You tap the camera button by the message input box.

Camera app opens. View of back camera is visible.
Screen shows your wheelchair in front of your unconscious friend.
Smartphone shows what the camera sees.
You move the wheelchair back with the mouth joystick.
Smartphone video zooms out to show full body of friend on ground.

Screen only shows the smartphone again.
You switch from photo to video mode.
Then you tap the record button to take a video of your friend lying
on the walkway.
(key tone)
(friend breathing)
You tap the record button again to stop recording.
(key tone)
To attach the video, you tap the use video button

Screen shows text chat again. One video is attached.
You tap the send button.
Screen shows file upload progress.
Attached video appears in chat thread.
The operator looks at the video.
The operator says: "I see the video. Help is on the way. If we need
more information, I will send you another message."

Image of flashing emergency lights.
Two minutes later, help comes.
