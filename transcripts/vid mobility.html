Using live video in an emergency.

Man in electric wheelchair going down a sidewalk.
Imagine you are in an electric wheelchair and you cannot move any of
your limbs.
On the way home you witness a parked car suddenly burst into flames from
across the street.

Screen shows back of man's head. There are headrest buttons and a
wheelchair-mounted smartphone.
You unlock your smartphone with the back headrest button.
Then you ask your voice assistant to call 911.

Smartphone with voice assistant appears.
Voice assistant, call 911.
Calling 911 in three seconds. Three, two, one.
The call is being made.
(phone ringing)

An emergency operator sees your medical ID profile.
You added your phone number and medical information in an emergency
caller database.
The emergency operator now knows you're quadriplegic

Smartphone with active 911 call.
An emergency operator answers the call.
The operator asks: "911. Do you need police, fire or medical?"
You say: "Fire."
The operator asks: "Where is your emergency?"
You say: "I am at Henning Drive and Gilmore Avenue in Burnaby."

An emergency operator sees your location on a map.
From the location data sent by your smartphone, the emergency operator
confirms your location.

Screen shows active call again.
The operator asks: "Is your phone number 604-473-9363?"
You say: "Yes."
The operator asks: "Are you Nathan Frost?"
You say: "Yes."

Your call is transferred to a fire call dispatcher.
The operator asks: "Burnaby fire. What is the nature of your emergency?"
You say: "A car is on fire."
The operator asks: "Are you in the car?"
You say: "No."
The operator asks: "Does your phone have a camera?"
You say: "Yes."
The operator says: "I am going to send a link for you to open so I can see
what is happening."
Text message notification appears.
You select and open the notification to see the message.

Text message app opens. There is one message with a link.
Hello, 911. Please open the link below so I can better assess the situation
and communicate with you.
You select and open the link.

Website appears with a permission dialog.
This website would like to access the microphone and camera. Cancel or allow.
You select allow.
Another dialog appears.
This website would like to use your current location. Cancel or allow.
You select allow.

Smartphone with active video call.
The video call has buttons to flip the camera, end the call, and send your
current location.
The video defaults to the back camera.
The operator says: "Okay, I got video now. Can you show me the car?"
You move the wheelchair to face the burning car with the headrest buttons.
Smartphone video shifts to the burning car.
The operator says: "Okay, I can see the car now."
The emergency operator notes the vehicle is a sedan.
There are no other hazards or people around and there is nobody inside the car.
The operator says: "Okay, I have dispatched firefighters and police. Please
stay clear of the area. Meanwhile, I will stay on the line in case there are
any new developments."

Image of flashing emergency lights.
Two minutes later, help comes.
