Using text messaging in an emergency.

Mom and son signing in a living room.
Imagine you are conversing with your mother using tactile sign language
when she suddenly collapsed.
(mother groaning)
Mother fallen over on sofa with hand on chest. She is in pain.
You can feel her body lying on the sofa, but she does not move.

Smartphone and braille device appears.
You open the phone dialer app with your braille device.

(electronic tone)
Phone dialer app opens. There is a phone keypad.
You type 911 in the phone number field and press enter.
Phone number, 911.

The call is being made.
(ringing tone)
An emergency operator sees your Medical ID profile.
You added your phone number and medical information in an emergency
caller database.
The emergency operator now knows you are deafblind and prefer texting.

Screen shows active call again.
The emergency operator sends you a text message.
(phone vibrating)
Text message notification appears.
Messages. Now, 911. Do you need police, fire, or medical?
You select the notification to reply to the message.

(electronic tone)
Text message app opens. There is one message.
You reply with your braille keyboard.
You reply: "Medical."
Message from 911: "Where is your emergency?"
You reply: "3999 Henning, Burnaby."
Message from 911: "Which floor are you on?"
You reply: "Ground floor."

An emergency operator sees your location on a map.
From the location you said, the operator now knows where you are.

Camera shows text chat again.
Message from 911: "Is your phone number 604-473-9363?"
You reply: "Yes."
Message from 911: "Are you Nathan Frost?"
You reply: "Yes."

Your call is transferred to a medical call dispatcher.
Message from 911: "Burnaby Medical. What is the nature of your emergency?"
You reply: "I was speaking with my mother when she suddenly fell down and
stopped moving."
Message from 911: "How old is your mother?"
You reply: "She is 62."
Message from 911: "Okay, I have dispatched an ambulance, and they will
be there soon."
Message from 911: "Meanwhile, can you attach a video showing your mother's
surroundings and her breathing?"
You select the camera button by the message input box.

Camera app opens. View of back camera is visible.
You move the camera until your mother is centered on screen.
The screen reader helps you aim the camera.
One face outside right edge. Near top edge. Center.
You switch from photo to video mode.
You select the record button to take a video of your mother lying on the sofa.
(phone beeps)
You select the record button again to stop recording.
(phone beeps)
To attach the video, you select the use video button.

Screen shows text chat again.
One video attached.
You select the send button.
Screen shows file upload progress.
Sending. 50%. Message sent.
Attached video appears in chat thread.
The operator looks at the video.
Message from 911: "I see the video."
Message from 911: "Help is on the way. If we need more information,
I will send you another message."

Image of flashing emergency lights.
Two minutes later, help comes.
