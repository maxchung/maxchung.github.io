Using voice, video, and text chat in an emergency.

Mom and son signing in a living room.
Imagine you are conversing with your mother using sign language when she
suddenly collapsed.
(mother groans)
Mother fallen over on sofa with hand on chest. She is in pain.
You shake her, but she does not move.

Smartphone appears.
You open the video chat app.
Video chat app opens. There is a new chat button.
You tap the new chat button.
New chat window opens. There is a "To" field and an "Add" button.
You type 911 in the To field and tap the Add button.
Audio call and video call buttons appear.
You tap the video call button.
Video call connecting screen appears.
(phone ringing)

An emergency operator sees your medical ID profile.
Your mother added your phone number and medical information in an emergency
caller database.
The emergency operator now knows you're deaf and prefers sign language.

Screen shows video chat app again.
An emergency operator and a video interpreter answer the video call.
Your face appears in the top left.
The emergency operator is an avatar in the top half of the screen.
The interpreter is in the bottom half.
There are buttons to flip the camera, end the call, and open the text chat.
Buttons to disable the microphone, speaker and camera are hidden in a more menu.

The emergency operator starts asking questions.
The operator asks: "911. Do you need police, fire, or medical?"
The video interpreter translates the question to sign language.
You use your other hand to sign your response.
The video interpreter voices your response for the emergency operator to hear.
The interpreter says: "Medical."
The process repeats for each question.
The operator asks: "Where is your emergency?"
(silence)
The interpreter says: "3999 Henning Burnaby."
The operator asks: "Which floor are you on?"
(silence)
The interpreter says: "Ground floor."

An emergency operator sees your location on a map.
From the location data sent by your smartphone, The emergency operator
confirms your location.

Screen shows video call again.
The operator asks: "Is your phone number (604) 473-9363?"
(silence)
The interpreter says: "Yes."
The operator asks: "Are you Nathan Frost?"
(silence)
The interpreter says: "Yes."

Your call is transferred to a medical call dispatcher.
The operator asks: "Burnaby Medical. What is the nature of your emergency?"
(silence)
The interpreter says: "I was speaking with my mother when she suddenly fell
down and stopped moving."
The operator asks: "How old is your mother?"
(silence)
The interpreter says: "She is 62."
The operator asks: "Can you switch to using your smartphone's rear camera?
There should be a flip camera button."
(silence)
You tap the flip camera button.
Caller's video switches to back camera.
The operator says: "Okay, good. Now with your phone camera, please show me
your mother."
(silence)

You hold the smartphone camera above your mother.
Bigger view of caller's video appears on the left.
Video shows mother lying on sofa.
The operator says: "Okay, I see your mother. Can you hold the phone close to
her face so I can hear her breathing?"
(silence)
Video shows mother's face.
The operator says: "Okay, I have dispatched an ambulance, and they will be
there soon. Meanwhile, I will stay on the line in case there are any
new developments."

Image of flashing emergency lights.
Two minutes later, help comes.
