Using real-time text in an emergency.

Man sitting in wheelchair in bedroom.
Imagine you are in an electric wheelchair and you cannot move any of
your limbs.
You are listening to an audio book at home when you hear a window break and
unfamiliar voices.

Screen shows man's head with headrest buttons and wheelchair-mounted smartphone.
The mouth joystick is hidden.
Smartphone appears.
You open the phone dialer app with the mouth joystick.
Phone dialer app opens. There is a phone keypad.
You dial 911 and press the call button.
You dial quickly to avoid alerting the intruders.
(keypad tones)
Three call options appear: Call, RTT/TTY call, and RTT/TTY relay call.
You select RTT/TTY call.
The call is being made.
(phone ringing)

Smartphone with active 911 call.
An emergency operator answers the call.
The RTT chat button is enabled.
You select the RTT button.

Text chat window appears.
Text typed is sent right away. There is no send button.
You can turn on the mic to speak.
The operator types: "911. Do you need police, fire or medical?"
You reply: "Police."
The operator types: "Where is your emergency?"
You reply: "I am at 3999 Henning Drive Burnaby in a room on the second floor."
Before you finish typing, the emergency operator read what you typed so far
and starts asking the next question.
The operator types: "Is your phone number 604-473-9363?"
You reply: "Yes."
The operator types: "What is your first and last name?"
You reply: "Nathan Frost."

Your call is transferred to a police call dispatcher.
The operator types: "Burnaby police. What is the nature of your emergency?"
You reply: "Someone broke into my home and they are downstairs."
The operator types: "Can you hide?"
You reply: "No, I am in a wheelchair."
The operator types: "Is there anyone else at home?"
You reply: "No."
The operator types: "What noises did you hear?"
You reply: "I heard glass breaking and unfamiliar voices."
The operator types: "Were there multiple voices?"
You reply: "Yes."
The operator types: "Were the strangers voices male or female?"
You reply: "Female."
The operator types: "Okay, I have dispatched police to your home and they
will be there soon."
The operator types: "Meanwhile, I will stay on the line in case there are
any new developments."

Image of flashing emergency lights.
Two minutes later, help comes.
